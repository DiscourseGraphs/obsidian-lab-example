## A short primer on candidate nodes


        - **Candidate Questions** are potential research questions to explore, of the scope to motivate an entire Project. #que-candidate
        - **Candidate Evidence** are equivalent to candidate results. This term might be preferred by those using the original Discourse Graph Dialect to perform literature synthesis (as opposed to the equivalent Results Graph dialect). The "Evidence" term  can also be used to disambiguate observations from the literature from Results from your own work.  #evd-candidate
        - **Candidate Hypotheses** are potential answers to the Question addressed by a particular project. They might live in the Resources field of a Project Page as you develop your expectations about the response to the intervention. #hyp-candidate
        - **Candidate Claims** are equivalent to to candidate hypotheses. This term might be preferred by those using the original Discourse Graph Dialect to perform literature synthesis (as opposed to the equivalent Results Graph dialect). The "Claim" term can also be used to disambiguate  proposed answers to a Question sourced from the literature from hypotheses that you've developed in the course of your work. #clm-candidate
        - **Candidate Issues** surface potential problems or future experiments. They may or may not be directly related to a particular Project. In a shared graph, an candidate issue can be a preliminary "Request for Experiments" that a lab group can refine together. #iss-candidate
